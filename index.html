Boeing 777: Dozens grounded after Denver engine failure

US plane manufacturer Boeing has recommended grounding all of the 777-model
aircraft which have the same type of engine that suffered failure and shed debris
over Denver on Saturday.
It said 128 jets should be suspended until inspections are carried out.
United Airlines and Japan's two main operators
have already stopped using 56 planes with the same engine.
Flight 328, carrying 231 passengers, was forced to make an emergency landing at
Denver airport.
No-one was injured.
"While [an] investigation is ongoing, we recommended suspending operations
of the 69 in-service and 59 in-storage 777 aircraft powered by
Pratt & Whitney 4000-112 engines,
"the company said in a statement.
